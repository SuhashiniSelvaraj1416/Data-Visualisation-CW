SARS-Cov-2 - CoVID19 Pandemic around the world

The new coronavirus, also known as COVID-19, first appeared in Wuhan, China in December 2019. By January 2020, the virus had spread to other countries and was declared a global health emergency by the World Health Organization (WHO). The virus spread rapidly around the world, causing widespread illness and death, and was declared a pandemic by WHO in March 2020. The virus continued to spread over the next few months, with many countries imposing lockdowns and other measures to slow the spread. Development and approval of an emergency vaccine will begin in late 2020, with commercialization efforts beginning in early 2021. As of March 2023, more than 8 billion doses of the COVID-19 vaccine have been administered worldwide. However, challenges such as vaccine hesitance and misinformation are hampering immunization efforts in some parts of the world. Despite challenges, the development and distribution of a COVID-19 vaccine has played a key role in containing the spread of the virus and mitigating its impact on global health and society.
